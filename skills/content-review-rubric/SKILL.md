# Content Review Rubric

Use this rubric before delegating a course review to your team.

## "Current" criteria
- References tools, frameworks, or practices still in active use today (not deprecated or superseded).
- Reflects how the topic is actually practiced in industry right now, not just textbook theory.
- Does not lean on examples or case studies that are more than a few years stale.

## "Engaging" criteria
- Mixes explanation with concrete examples, stories, or interactive exercises — not just dense prose.
- Breaks long topics into short, digestible sections.
- Avoids walls of text with no visuals, code, or activities to break up the reading.

## How to brief your reviewers
- Give both reviewers the same course topic and outline/content.
- Tell the Trend Researcher to focus only on currency (the "Current" criteria above).
- Tell the Engagement Reviewer to focus only on engagement (the "Engaging" criteria above).
- When both reports come back, synthesize them into one verdict: what to add/update for currency, what to rewrite for engagement, and an overall recommendation (ship as-is / revise / overhaul).
